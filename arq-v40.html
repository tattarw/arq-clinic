What's inside

Four pillars. One membership.

The health check you were supposed to have.

Not the 12 markers your annual physical runs. A full read across cardiac, metabolic, hormonal, inflammatory, nutrient and cellular systems — drawn at home, processed at NABL-accredited labs.

How is this different from a standard physical?
An annual physical covers 10–15 markers. arq. runs 100+. You'll see risk signals years before a physical would flag them — and your physician can act on them.
What exactly do you test?
Six systems: cardiac, metabolic, hormonal, inflammatory, nutrient and cellular. The full marker list is in your dashboard after your first draw.
Where is the draw done?
At home in 40+ Indian cities, or at any NABL-accredited partner centre. You pick.

Your biology, read over time.

Upload past labs. Connect Apple Health, WHOOP or Oura. See your biology as a trajectory — not a single Tuesday in August.

How is my data used?
Only to build and refine your protocol with your physician. Never sold. Never shared without consent.
How secure is my data?
Encrypted at rest and in transit, on DPDP-compliant Indian servers. Export or delete anytime.

A plan for you. Literally.

Every recommendation — diet, training, supplements, prescription — tied to a specific marker or goal. No generic wellness. No templates.

How do labs turn into a protocol?
Your physician reviews your panel within 72 hours, books a consult with you, and issues your protocol through the arq. app.
How is this different from generic health advice?
Every line in your protocol cites the marker or goal it's tied to. If we can't justify it, we don't prescribe it.

A physician, assigned. Not rotated.

The same doctor every time. Replies in under two hours. Consult when you need. Adjust when your data moves.

Who do I actually speak with?
A licensed Indian physician assigned to you, plus a human care lead. The same people every time — no rotations, no call centres.
Is this another chatbot?
No. AI helps us prep and summarise. Every clinical decision is made by your physician.
