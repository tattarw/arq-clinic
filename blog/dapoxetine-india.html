Dapoxetine in India: The Only Approved. PE Medication

April 16, 2026
arq. medical team
Medically reviewed

Premature ejaculation affects 20% of men. It's common, treatable, and nothing to be ashamed of. Yet most men suffer in silence rather than talk to a doctor. Dapoxetine (brand names Priligy, Duralast in India) is the first and only FDA-approved medication specifically designed for PE — taken on-demand, 1 hours before intercourse, it delays ejaculation 3-4x on average. But like all medications, it requires proper dosing, understanding of side effects, and baseline. bloodwork to ensure safety.

Key takeaways

Dapoxetine basics

  • What it is A short-acting SSRI (selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor) designed for premature ejaculation, not depression
  • How it works Increases serotonin levels, delays signal to ejaculate, increasing intravaginal ejaculation latency time (IELT) 3-4x
  • Dosing 30-60mg taken 1 hours before intercourse (not daily)
  • Efficacy 60% of men experience meaningful improvement (IELT increase ≥3x). Works on first dose in most men.
  • Available in India Duralast, Priligy generics. Cost:
  • Bloodwork before Baseline serotonin status, thyroid, testosterone, liver function (dapoxetine is metabolized hepatically)

What is Premature Ejaculation?

Premature ejaculation is defined clinically as ejaculation occurring <1 minute after penetration, in ≥75% of sexual encounters, for ≥6 months. But more importantly, it's when. the man feels he lacks control — his ejaculation happens sooner than he or his partner wants.

Causes vary

  • Psychological Anxiety (performance anxiety, relationship stress, trauma), depression, low self-esteem
  • Neurochemical Low serotonin (serotonin delays ejaculation; low serotonin = earlier ejaculation)
  • Physiological Thyroid dysfunction, hormonal imbalance, prostate inflammation
  • Mixed Usually a combination

This is why testing matters. A man with low testosterone and performance anxiety needs both hormonal support and behavioral tools — not just dapoxetine alone.

How Dapoxetine Works

Dapoxetine is a short-acting selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor (SSRI). Here's the mechanism

Normal ejaculation Serotonin levels are low → brain signal to ejaculate fires → ejaculation happens (usually within 1 minute).

With dapoxetine Dapoxetine blocks serotonin reuptake → serotonin levels spike → brain's ejaculation signal is delayed → ejaculation takes 3-4x longer (3 minutes on average).

Why short-acting? Long-acting SSRIs (sertraline, paroxetine) are taken daily for depression and have many systemic side effects. Dapoxetine has a half-life of ~1.4 hours — you take it 1 hours before intercourse, it peaks in your bloodstream right when you need it, then clears out. This minimizes side effects and allows on-demand dosing.

Efficacy Increases intravaginal ejaculation latency time (IELT) by 3-4x on average. A man who normally lasts 1 minute might last 3 minutes with dapoxetine. Not indefinite control, but meaningful improvement in most men.

Dosing and Timing in India

Standard doses 30mg or 60mg tablets

  • Start with 30mg Take 1 hours before sexual activity. If tolerated but ineffective, move to 60mg.
  • 60mg More effective but more side effects (nausea, dizziness). Only for men who don't respond to 30mg.
  • Maximum frequency Once per 24 hours (don't take it multiple times in one day)
  • With or without food Can take with food, though food may slightly delay absorption

Available brands in India

  • Duralast 30mg or 60mg,(OTC in many pharmacies)
  • Priligy (brand)(less common, more expensive)
  • Generic dapoxetine(various manufacturers)

How to use Plan intercourse 1 hours after taking dapoxetine. Timing is crucial — take it too early and it clears out; too late and it won't be peak yet. Trial helps you find your personal sweet spot.

Efficacy: What the Studies Show

Multiple randomized controlled trials (RCTs) have tested dapoxetine

PRISM trials (large, placebo-controlled) 60% of men on dapoxetine achieved clinically meaningful improvement (IELT ≥3x increase). About 35% saw no significant benefit. This means roughly 2 in 3 men benefit; 1 in 3 don't.

Responders vs. non-responders Men with lower baseline serotonin, higher anxiety, and younger age tend to respond better. Men with severe comorbid ED or long-standing PE may respond less.

Works immediately Unlike daily SSRIs (which take 2 weeks to work), dapoxetine can show effect on the first dose. Most men notice benefit within 1 uses.

Side Effects: Real and Rare

Common (15%)

  • Nausea Most common, especially at 60mg. Usually mild and transient.
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness Occurs in 10% at 30mg, higher at 60mg. Can impair driving or balance.
  • Headache 5%, mild to moderate
  • Insomnia or somnolence Rare; happens in <5%
  • Dry mouth
  • Erectile dysfunction Paradoxically, some men report transient ED (dapoxetine can reduce erectile rigidity in a minority). Usually mild and temporary.

Rare but serious (<1%)

  • Syncope (fainting) Especially if taken with alcohol or on an empty stomach
  • Serotonin syndrome Only if combined with other serotonergic drugs (certain antidepressants, tramadol). Symptoms: agitation, confusion, rapid heart rate, muscle rigidity.
  • Hepatotoxicity Rare; liver dysfunction has been reported in isolated cases

Management Most side effects improve with repeated use. If nausea persists, take with food or switch to lower dose. If dizziness is severe, don't drive or operate machinery.

Bloodwork Before Starting Dapoxetine

Not optional. Here's why

1. Baseline Serotonin Status (if available)
Expensive and not routine, but some labs can measure serotonin levels. Helps predict response to dapoxetine.

2. Thyroid Panel (TSH, Free T4)
Thyroid dysfunction causes sexual dysfunction. Hypothyroidism can worsen PE. Must be ruled out or treated.

3. Testosterone (Total & Free)
Low testosterone impairs sexual function and is common in Indian men. If testosterone is low, PE medication alone won't fix it — you need hormone optimization alongside.

4. Liver Function Tests (AST, ALT, GGT)
Dapoxetine is metabolized by the liver via CYP3A4 and CYP2D6. If you have liver disease or cirrhosis, dapoxetine is contraindicated.

5. Prolactin
High prolactin causes sexual dysfunction. Must be checked.

6. Blood Pressure
Dapoxetine can lower BP slightly. Baseline measurement is important if you're already on antihypertensives.

7. Complete Blood Count (CBC)
Baseline safety check.

Combinations: Dapoxetine + Other Drugs

Dapoxetine + PDE5 Inhibitors (Sildenafil/Tadalafil)

Standard and safe. Many men have both PE and ED. Dapoxetine delays ejaculation; sildenafil improves erectile rigidity. Together, they address both problems. Cost: dapoxetine + sildenafil per encounter. Requires physician prescription due to drug interaction monitoring.

Dapoxetine + Behavioral Therapy (Stop-Start Technique)

Combines medication with psychology. Stop-start technique: during masturbation, when approaching climax, stop stimulation until the urge subsides, then resume. Over weeks, this builds lasting control. Dapoxetine + therapy works better than either alone.

Dapoxetine + Topical Anesthetic (5% Lidocaine Spray)

Some men use dapoxetine + lidocaine spray (applied to penis 15 minutes before) for additional delay. Synergistic effect, but verify with your physician — may reduce sensation too much for some partners.

Avoid: Dapoxetine + Other SSRIs or SNRIs

Do NOT combine dapoxetine with sertraline, paroxetine, or venlafaxine. Risk of serotonin syndrome. If you're on a daily SSRI for depression/anxiety, you usually don't need dapoxetine (the daily SSRI already delays ejaculation).

Who Shouldn't Take Dapoxetine?

  • Heart disease or uncontrolled arrhythmia Dapoxetine can affect cardiac conduction
  • Severe liver disease or cirrhosis Can't metabolize it safely
  • Hypersensitivity to dapoxetine or other SSRIs Allergic reaction
  • Current therapy with other serotonergic agents Risk of serotonin syndrome
  • Uncontrolled depression or psychosis SSRI use requires psychiatric oversight
  • Patients on QT-prolonging drugs Dapoxetine can slightly prolong QT interval

What If Dapoxetine Doesn't Work?

Try 60mg if 30mg didn't work. Some men need the higher dose.

If still no benefit after 3 uses at proper timing Your PE may be primarily psychological (anxiety-driven) rather than neurochemical. Next steps

  • Behavioral therapy Stop-start technique, sensate focus exercises, cognitive-behavioral therapy for sexual anxiety
  • Long-term SSRI Sertraline 25-50mg daily or paroxetine 10-20mg daily (different mechanism — daily suppression rather than on-demand)
  • Combination Dapoxetine + therapy often succeeds when either alone fails
  • Reassess underlying health Is your testosterone low? Thyroid off? Relationship stress? These all factor in.

Questions

Frequently asked about Dapoxetine

  • How does dapoxetine differ from SSRIs like sertraline?
    Dapoxetine is a short-acting SSRI (half-life ~1.4 hours) designed specifically for PE — you take it 1 hours before intercourse, not daily. Sertraline is long-acting (taken daily for depression/anxiety). Both delay ejaculation via serotonin reuptake inhibition, but dapoxetine's short window of action makes it practical for on-demand use. Long-term SSRIs also delay ejaculation but have more side effects with daily dosing.
  • What is IELT and why does it matter?
    IELT (Intravaginal Ejaculation Latency Time) is how long a man lasts after penetration begins. PE is defined as IELT <1 minute. Dapoxetine increases IELT by 3-4x on average — from ~1 minute to 3 minutes, sometimes longer. This is measured in randomized controlled trials and is the clinical benchmark for PE efficacy.
  • Can I take dapoxetine with erectile dysfunction meds?
    Yes. Dapoxetine + sildenafil (Viagra) or tadalafil (Cialis) is a standard combination for men with PE + ED. The two drugs work via different mechanisms — dapoxetine delays ejaculation (neurological), PDE5 inhibitors improve erectile rigidity (vascular). Combined, they address both problems. Cost: dapoxetine + sildenafil . Your physician determines safe dosing.
  • What happens if I miss a dose or skip a day?
    Dapoxetine is taken on-demand, so skipping a dose means no medication that encounter. Its short half-life (1.4 hours) means no cumulative effects — it's only active for 2 hours post-dose. If you don't take it, you won't get the benefit that session. There's no 'catch-up' dosing; just take it next time.
  • Does dapoxetine work on the first dose?
    Yes, in most men. Efficacy can be seen immediately (first dose), though some men notice better results after 2 uses (psychological adaptation). About 60% of men see clinically meaningful improvement (IELT increase of ≥3x) with dapoxetine. Response varies by baseline IELT, anxiety level, and individual neurochemistry.
  • Is dapoxetine addictive or does tolerance develop?
    No addiction risk — dapoxetine is not a controlled substance and has no abuse potential. Tolerance (reduced efficacy over time) is rare; most men maintain effect with consistent use. Psychological dependence is possible if anxiety becomes tied to the medication, but true physiological tolerance doesn't happen with dapoxetine's mechanism.
  • What if dapoxetine doesn't work for me?
    Try 60mg if you started with 30mg — higher dose helps some men. If still no benefit after 3 uses at proper timing, your PE may be psychological (anxiety-driven) rather than neurochemical. Your physician can explore: behavioral therapy (stop-start technique), topical anesthetics (5% lidocaine spray), or long-term SSRIs. Combination approaches often work better than medication alone.
  • Does dapoxetine affect fertility or contraception?
    No effect on fertility, sperm quality, or partners' contraception. Dapoxetine is not systemic enough (short half-life) to affect reproductive hormones. If your partner is on oral contraceptives, dapoxetine doesn't interfere. Safe to use with all contraception methods. If fertility is a concern, discuss with your physician — most PE treatments don't impair it.
