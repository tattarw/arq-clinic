NAD+ Supplements in India:What Doctors Actually Say

April 16, 2026
arq. medical team
Medically reviewed

NAD+ is the new longevity darling. Biohackers and anti-aging clinics are pushing NMN capsules (), NAD+ IV infusions (), and nicotinamide riboside (NR) as the shortcut to staying young. The science in mice is compelling — restoring NAD+ extends lifespan and reverses aging hallmarks. But here's the honest truth: human data is sparse. Marketing promises vastly outpace evidence. And there are proven interventions (sleep, exercise, fasting) that boost NAD+ naturally, cost nothing, and have robust human evidence. Before you spend annually on NAD+ supplements, understand what you're actually buying.

What Is NAD+ and Why It Matters

NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is a coenzyme — a helper molecule essential for cellular function. Every cell in your body uses NAD+ for

  • Energy production Mitochondrial ATP synthesis. More NAD+ = more cellular energy.
  • DNA repair NAD+-dependent enzymes (PARPs, sirtuins) repair DNA damage from radiation, oxidative stress, aging.
  • Circadian rhythm regulation NAD+ levels oscillate with circadian cycles. Disrupted NAD+ = disrupted sleep, metabolism, aging.
  • Cellular stress response Sirtuins (NAD+-dependent proteins) coordinate longevity pathways, autophagy, metabolic switching.
  • NAD+ depletion with aging NAD+ levels decline sharply — at 50 years old, most men have 50% less NAD+ than at 20. This decline is associated with Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, cardiovascular disease, metabolic dysfunction.

The theory: restoring NAD+ could reverse aging. Animal studies suggest this is true.

The Animal Evidence: Mice Live Longer

Preclinical data on NAD+ is extraordinarily compelling. Mice supplemented with NMN or NR show

  • Extended lifespan 10% longer than controls
  • Improved metabolic health Better glucose control, increased insulin sensitivity
  • Enhanced mitochondrial function Restored energy production in aging tissues
  • Reduced inflammation Lower inflammatory markers (IL TNF-alpha)
  • Improved cardiovascular function Better endothelial function, reduced atherosclerosis
  • Restored cognitive function Improved memory and spatial learning in aging mouse brains
  • Enhanced physical. performance Improved exercise capacity, muscle strength

These effects are real in mouse models. But here's the critical caveat:mice are not humans. What works in a controlled 2-year mouse lifespan may not translate to human longevity. Mice don't have lifestyle complexity, stress, poor sleep, sedentary behavior — the real-world factors driving aging in men.

The Human Evidence: Modest and Limited

In humans, NAD+ research is nascent. Here's what the few human studies show

Small Pilot Studies (2 weeks)

  • NMN or NR supplementation (500 mg daily) increases circulating NAD+ levels by 10%
  • Effects are modest. Increases persist only while supplementing.
  • Intracellular NAD+ (what actually matters for biology) increases less reliably than circulating NAD+

Metabolic Markers

Some early studies report modest improvements in glucose control, insulin sensitivity, or lipid profiles after NAD+ supplementation. But

  • Sample sizes are tiny (20 subjects)
  • Studies lack robust controls
  • Effect sizes are small (1% improvements)
  • No long-term follow-up (most studies < 6 months)

The Gap: No Human Longevity Data

There are zero human randomized controlled trials showing that NAD+ supplementation extends lifespan or improves healthspan. None. The longest human studies are 12 weeks. We don't know if NAD+ supplements actually make men live longer or age slower.

This is the critical point: animal evidence is compelling, but translating to humans is unproven.

The Bioavailability Problem

There's a fundamental biopharmaceutical issue with oral NAD+ precursors: poor absorption.

When you swallow NMN or NR (nicotinamide riboside) capsules, they must

  1. Survive stomach acid
  2. Cross the intestinal epithelium (which has active NAD+-degrading enzymes)
  3. Enter your bloodstream
  4. Cross the cell membrane
  5. Reach mitochondria to be converted back to NAD+

Current estimates:only 1% of oral NAD+ precursor actually reaches cells as NAD+. The rest is degraded or lost in absorption.

This is why NAD+ IV infusions show more dramatic blood NAD+ spikes — they bypass the intestinal barrier. But IV spikes are temporary (2 hours) and return to baseline once the body metabolizes the infusion.

Urolithin A & Other "NAD-boosting" Compounds

Emerging supplements claim to boost NAD+. Urolithin A (metabolite of pomegranate/berry polyphenols) is getting attention. But human evidence is very limited. Avoid until data is stronger.

What Actually Boosts NAD+ (Naturally, Free)

Here's what has robust human evidence for increasing NAD+ levels naturally

Sleep (7 Hours, Consistent)

NAD+ synthesis is highest during sleep. One night of poor sleep tanks NAD+ levels. Circadian alignment is critical. If your sleep is broken, no supplement will compensate.

Resistance Training

Strength training activates SIRT1/SIRT3 (NAD+-dependent proteins) and upregulates NAD+ synthesis. 3 sessions weekly, moderate-high intensity.

High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT)

Intense sprints or intervals activate sirtuins and NAD+ metabolism faster than steady-state cardio.

Fasting & Time-Restricted Eating

Short-term fasting (12 hour overnight fast, or 24-hour fast 1x weekly) activates sirtuins and increases NAD+ turnover. Intermittent fasting shows robust NAD+ increases in humans.

Caloric Restriction (Mild)

Eating 10% fewer calories activates longevity pathways. Combined with intermittent fasting, effects are additive.

Cold Exposure

Cold showers or ice baths activate brown adipose tissue (BAT) and NAD+-dependent thermogenesis. 10 min cold exposure increases NAD+ acutely and chronically with repeated exposure.

Heat Stress (Sauna)

Paradoxically, sauna (heat stress) also activates stress-response pathways including NAD+ metabolism. 20 min, 80°C, 2-3x weekly.

Polyphenol-Rich Foods

Red grapes, berries, dark chocolate, green tea, red wine contain resveratrol and other polyphenols that activate sirtuins (NAD+-dependent). These are indirect NAD+ boosters.

Adequate B3 (Niacin)

NAD+ is synthesized from B3 (niacin, nicotinamide). RDA is 14 mg daily; most diets provide this. Food sources: chicken, tuna, peanuts, mushrooms. Mega-dosing B3 (500+ mg) can increase NAD+ but has side effects (flushing, nausea).

Questions

Frequently asked about NAD+ supplements

  • What is NAD+ and why does it matter?
    NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is a coenzyme found in every cell. It's essential for mitochondrial energy production (ATP generation), DNA repair, circadian rhythm regulation, and cellular stress response. NAD+ levels decline sharply with age — by 50 years old, most men have 50% less NAD+ than in their 20s. This decline is associated with aging, metabolic dysfunction, and disease (Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, cardiovascular disease, diabetes). Restoring NAD+ levels is theorized to reverse aging hallmarks. The science in animals is compelling; human data lags behind.
  • Do NAD+ supplements (NMN, NR) actually increase NAD+ levels?
    This is where the science gets murky. Animal studies (mice, rats) show that NMN and NR supplementation increases intracellular NAD+ and improves markers of aging, metabolic function, and longevity. In humans, small pilot studies show increases in circulating NAD+ after supplementation, but the increases are modest (10%), and translating this to meaningful health outcomes is unclear. NAD+ IV infusions show more dramatic increases in blood NAD+ (short-lived, lasting hours). Oral supplements are poorly absorbed — estimates suggest only 1% bioavailability. The gap between animal and human data is significant.
  • What NAD+ supplements are available in India?

NR (nicotinamide riboside): Less available but available from some suppliers, similar pricing. NAD+ IV infusions:. Available at premium wellness clinics and some integrative medicine practitioners in major cities. Most reputable option but temporary (NAD+ returns to baseline within hours after infusion). Urolithin A (metabolite of pomegranate/berries): Emerging supplement, poorly studied in humans. Avoid until human data is robust.
  • Should I take NAD+ supplements for longevity?
    Honest answer: premature. The human clinical evidence for NAD+ supplementation improving healthspan or lifespan is limited. Animal data is compelling — mice on NMN/NR live longer and healthier. But mice are not humans. Cost-benefit analysis: NAD+ supplements cost . You'd spend annually on something with modest human evidence. Meanwhile, interventions with robust human evidence (sleep optimization, resistance training, Mediterranean diet, stress management, cold exposure) cost and deliver superior results. Do these first. NAD+ supplements might be added once foundational longevity behaviors are established.
  • What interventions actually boost NAD+ naturally?
    Sleep (consistent 7 hours): NAD+ synthesis is highest during sleep. One night of poor sleep tanks NAD+ levels. Circadian alignment is critical. Exercise (especially resistance training and high-intensity intervals): Both activate SIRT proteins and NAD+-dependent pathways. Fasting and time-restricted eating: Short-term fasting activates sirtuins and NAD+ metabolism. Even 12 hour overnight fasts show effects. Caloric restriction: Mild calorie deficit activates NAD+-dependent longevity pathways. Cold exposure: Activates brown adipose tissue and NAD+-dependent thermogenesis. Sauna/heat stress: Also activates these pathways (though opposite stimulus). Polyphenol-rich foods (red grapes, berries, tea): Compounds like resveratrol activate sirtuins. These have human evidence and cost nothing.
  • Are NAD+ IV infusions worth the cost?
    Short-term boost, high cost. NAD+ IV infusions create a dramatic but temporary spike in blood NAD+ (lasting 2 hours). The effect is real but fleeting — NAD+ returns to baseline once the infusion metabolizes. per session. Frequency: Often sold as weekly or biweekly sessions for lasting effect. Annual . This is expensive for something with limited evidence of sustained benefit in humans. NAD+ oral supplements offer slower, cheaper alternatives (if you're committed). IV is reserved for research settings or wealthy biohackers willing to experiment.
  • What bloodwork should I get before considering NAD+ supplements?
    Baseline metabolic assessment includes: fasting glucose, insulin, A1C (metabolic health), lipid panel (cardiovascular health), inflammatory markers (CRP, IL), liver and kidney function (processing NAD+ precursors), vitamin B3 status (NAD+ is synthesized from B3), mitochondrial function markers (CoQ10 levels if indicated), and homocysteine (marker of aging). Additionally, if considering IV NAD+, baseline blood pressure and cardiac status are important. Repeat testing at 3 months on NAD+ supplementation would show whether metabolic markers improve. Many men supplement without these baselines — you're essentially guessing whether NAD+ supplementation is helping.
  • What about NAD+ precursors like tryptophan and nicotinamide?
    Tryptophan (amino acid) is converted to NAD+ via the kynurenine pathway (de novo NAD synthesis). Sufficient dietary tryptophan is required. But tryptophan alone doesn't dramatically boost NAD+ — the conversion is rate-limited by other factors. Nicotinamide (niacinamide, a form of B3) is another NAD+ precursor. Dietary sources include chicken, tuna, peanuts, mushrooms. RDA for vitamin B3 is 14 mg daily; most diet provides this. Supplementing B3 beyond RDA (500+ mg daily) can increase NAD+, but mega-dosing has side effects (flushing, nausea, liver toxicity at very high doses). NMN and NR are more efficient NAD+ precursors than tryptophan or B3, but bioavailability remains the limiting factor.
