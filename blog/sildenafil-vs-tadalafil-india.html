Sildenafil vs Tadalafil: Which ED Medication Is Right for You? (Doctor's Guide)

April 16, 2026
arq. medical team
Medically reviewed

Most men choose between Sildenafil and Tadalafil without knowing what matters: the bloodwork that reveals whether you actually need medication, and whether it will work. These two medications are the most prescribed ED treatments globally, available in India as generics for as little as 50 INR per dose. But choosing between them means understanding not just how they work, but which fits your lifestyle, your medical history, and your goal. One isn't universally "better" — it depends on you.

Key differences at a glance

Sildenafil vs Tadalafil: Quick comparison

  • Sildenafil (Viagra/Vigore): Onset 30-60 min, duration 4-6 hours, take as-needed 1 hour before sex, affected by food, 50-150 INR per tablet
  • Tadalafil (Cialis/Tadacip): Onset 30 min, duration up to 36 hours, option for daily 5mg OR as-needed 20mg, food-independent, 80-200 INR per tablet
  • Both are PDE5 inhibitors — they relax smooth muscle in penile arteries, increasing blood flow during sexual stimulation
  • Neither will work if your testosterone is low or if you have diabetes without glucose control — that's why blood tests come first
  • Choice depends on lifestyle (spontaneous vs planned), side effect tolerance, and whether you want daily dosing (Tadalafil 5mg) or on-demand (both medications)

How PDE5 Inhibitors Work: The Simple Explanation

To understand Sildenafil and Tadalafil, you need to know what happens during erectile dysfunction.

In a healthy erection, sexual stimulation triggers the release of nitric oxide in penile tissue. Nitric oxide activates an enzyme called guanylate cyclase, which increases levels of a messenger molecule called cGMP. Elevated cGMP relaxes smooth muscle in penile arteries, blood flow increases, and an erection happens.

The problem: an enzyme called phosphodiesterase-5 (PDE5) breaks down cGMP. When PDE5 is overactive or when cardiovascular disease or hormonal dysfunction limits nitric oxide production, cGMP drops — and the erection fails.

PDE5 inhibitors (Sildenafil and Tadalafil) block this enzyme. They allow cGMP to accumulate, keeping smooth muscle relaxed and blood flow sustained. But — and this is critical — they only work if there's sexual stimulation and if your vascular system and hormones are intact. If your testosterone is low, your arteries are damaged, or your glucose is uncontrolled, the medication has a harder time working. This is why bloodwork is non-negotiable.

Both Sildenafil and Tadalafil work the same way. The differences are in pharmacokinetics — how fast they work, how long they last, and how your body metabolizes them.

Sildenafil (Viagra, Vigore): The Classic Choice

How It Works

Sildenafil is a short-acting PDE5 inhibitor. Take it 30-60 minutes before sex, and it peaks in your blood within 1-2 hours.

Onset: 30-60 minutes (faster on an empty stomach, slower after a heavy meal)

Duration: 4-6 hours (it leaves your body relatively quickly)

Dosing: 50mg or 100mg, taken as-needed before sexual activity

Key Characteristics

Food interaction: Sildenafil is significantly affected by food. A high-fat meal delays onset by 30-40 minutes and reduces peak concentration. Take it on an empty stomach or after a light meal for best results. This is the major disadvantage compared to Tadalafil.

Predictable window: If you take Sildenafil at 9 PM, it will be gone by 3 AM. This is an advantage if you want a defined window and prefer the drug to wear off completely.

Repeated dosing: You can take it multiple times daily if needed, though most men take it once per occasion. Each dose is independent.

Side Effects

  • Headache (most common, 20-30% of men) — caused by vasodilation and usually mild
  • Flushing — facial redness, usually mild and transient
  • Indigestion — dyspepsia, especially with food
  • Nasal congestion — mild stuffiness
  • Dizziness — especially if combined with alcohol or blood pressure medication
  • Transient visual disturbance (rare, <1%) — blue-tinted vision at high doses

Most side effects are mild and decrease with repeated use as your body tolerates the medication.

Cost in India

Brand (Viagra): 500-800 INR per 100mg tablet
Generics (Vigore, Manforce, Kamagra): 50-150 INR per 50-100mg tablet

Generic Sildenafil is bioequivalent to brand Viagra — same active drug, different manufacturer. Always verify your pharmacy dispenses licensed generics from DCGI-approved manufacturers.

Who Sildenafil Suits Best

  • Men who have planned sexual encounters (can take it 1 hour before)
  • Men who prefer a defined window (knowing it will wear off in 6 hours)
  • Men with jobs requiring alertness (it clears completely overnight)
  • Men who experience side effects from longer-acting drugs

Tadalafil (Cialis, Tadacip): The Flexible Option

How It Works

Tadalafil is a long-acting PDE5 inhibitor with a half-life of 17.5 hours. This means half of each dose is still in your system at 17.5 hours, and traces persist for 36+ hours.

Onset: 30 minutes (same as Sildenafil, but faster and more reliable)

Duration: Up to 36 hours (hence the name "the weekend pill")

Dosing options: Two strategies —

  • As-needed 20mg: Take 1 hour before sex, lasts 36 hours, same as Sildenafil in terms of timing but with much longer duration
  • Daily 5mg: Take every day, regardless of sexual activity. Maintains steady state, allowing spontaneous sex without timing medication around encounters

Key Characteristics

Food-independent: Tadalafil works equally well after food or on an empty stomach. This is a major advantage — you don't have to plan around meals. Have dinner, take the medication, and expect it to work as expected.

Extended window: The 36-hour duration means one dose covers you for an entire weekend. Take it Friday evening, and you're covered through Sunday. This appeals to men who like spontaneity and unpredictability.

Daily dosing option: Low-dose Tadalafil (5mg daily) keeps you in a constant state of PDE5 suppression. This option is ideal for men with frequent sexual activity (2+ times per week) or men who want to "set it and forget it" without timing considerations.

Side Effects

  • Back pain or muscle aches (10-15% of men, unique to Tadalafil) — usually mild and dose-dependent, worse with 20mg than 5mg daily
  • Headache (13-15% of men) — similar to Sildenafil
  • Indigestion — less common than with Sildenafil since food doesn't affect absorption
  • Nasal congestion — mild, similar incidence to Sildenafil
  • Flushing — less common than Sildenafil
  • Dizziness — especially if combined with alcohol

The back pain is unique and noteworthy. It's more common at higher doses and typically mild, but some men find it intolerable. If you experience back pain on as-needed 20mg, switching to daily 5mg often reduces or eliminates it.

Cost in India

Brand (Cialis): 600-1000 INR per 20mg tablet or 400-700 INR per 5mg tablet
Generics (Tadacip, Tadalis, Tadalista): 80-200 INR per 20mg, 60-120 INR per 5mg

Generic Tadalafil is bioequivalent — same drug, lower cost. Daily 5mg is often cheaper per month than as-needed 20mg if you use it frequently.

Who Tadalafil Suits Best

  • Men who have frequent and unpredictable sexual activity (want spontaneity without timing medication)
  • Men who eat heavily before sex (food doesn't affect it)
  • Men who prefer once-a-week dosing (20mg as-needed) or daily medication (5mg)
  • Men with jobs or lifestyles requiring sustained activity across a weekend
  • Men who tolerate longer drug duration (36 hours in system is acceptable to them)

Why Blood Tests Matter BEFORE Prescribing

Here's what most men don't know: 40% of men under 40 with erectile dysfunction have low testosterone or undiagnosed diabetes. The PDE5 inhibitor alone won't fix that — it treats the symptom (penile hemodynamics), not the cause (hormonal dysfunction or metabolic disease).

Taking Sildenafil or Tadalafil without knowing what's driving your ED is like taking a headache pill for a brain tumor. The medication might work for the immediate problem, but you're ignoring the underlying issue.

Essential Bloodwork Before ED Medication

1. Testosterone (Total and Free)
Low testosterone causes ED. If your testosterone is below 300 ng/dL, a PDE5 inhibitor alone will have limited efficacy. You need testosterone replacement (TRT) or clomiphene to restore hormone levels. Many men see their ED resolve once testosterone is optimized.

2. Prolactin
Elevated prolactin inhibits erectile function and libido. High prolactin can be from a pituitary tumor, dopamine antagonists (antipsychotics), or unknown causes. If it's high, treating the cause (medication adjustment, pituitary imaging) is essential before PDE5 inhibitors.

3. Fasting Glucose and HbA1c
Diabetes damages blood vessels and nerves controlling erections. Uncontrolled glucose (HbA1c >7%) makes ED medication less effective. Optimal glucose control is required for good outcomes.

4. Lipid Panel (Cholesterol, LDL, HDL, Triglycerides)
High cholesterol and elevated triglycerides indicate endothelial dysfunction — the very mechanism driving ED. Statin therapy improves ED outcomes alongside PDE5 inhibitors.

5. Thyroid Function (TSH, Free T4)
Hypothyroidism causes ED. Thyroid replacement improves erectile function and should be initiated before PDE5 inhibitors if present.

6. Renal Function (Creatinine, eGFR)
Both Sildenafil and Tadalafil are metabolized by the kidneys. If your kidneys are compromised, medication clearance is delayed — dosing needs adjustment.

Sildenafil vs Tadalafil: Efficacy and Response Rates

In clinical trials, both medications have similar efficacy: approximately 70-80% of men report improved erections. However, individual response varies significantly.

Factors affecting efficacy:

  • Testosterone levels: Men with T >500 ng/dL respond better than those with low-normal testosterone
  • Vascular health: Men without cardiovascular disease respond better than those with coronary artery disease, hypertension, or diabetes
  • Diabetes control: HbA1c <7% is associated with better medication response
  • Duration of ED: Newer-onset ED (months) responds better than chronic (years); suggests hormonal or psychogenic causes are more treatable
  • Body weight and BMI: Obesity and metabolic syndrome reduce PDE5 inhibitor efficacy

Many men who think they're "non-responders" to PDE5 inhibitors actually have underlying metabolic or hormonal dysfunction. Once these are treated, medication efficacy improves dramatically.

Safety: Contraindications and Drug Interactions

Absolute Contraindications

Concurrent nitrate use (CRITICAL): Nitroglycerin, isosorbide dinitrate, and other nitrates are used for angina. Combining them with Sildenafil or Tadalafil causes catastrophic blood pressure drop and can be fatal. This is non-negotiable — if you're on nitrates, you cannot use PDE5 inhibitors.

Major Drug Interactions

Alpha-blockers (Tamsulosin, Doxazosin): Used for benign prostate hyperplasia or high blood pressure. Combined with PDE5 inhibitors, they cause significant blood pressure drop. If you're on an alpha-blocker, your physician must adjust your protocol carefully — sometimes switching medications or spacing doses.

Antifungals (Ketoconazole, Itraconazole): These inhibit the enzyme (CYP3A4) that metabolizes both Sildenafil and Tadalafil. Combining them can dangerously increase drug concentration. Dose adjustment is required.

Protease inhibitors (for HIV): Similar enzyme inhibition — careful dosing required.

Riociguat: A pulmonary hypertension drug. Absolute contraindication with both PDE5 inhibitors.

Relative Precautions

Blood pressure medications (ACE inhibitors, beta-blockers, calcium channel blockers): Additive blood pressure-lowering effect. Not a contraindication, but your blood pressure should be monitored. If you're on multiple antihypertensives, physician oversight is important.

Alcohol: Enhances blood pressure drop, increases dizziness, headache, and flushing risk. Light alcohol (1-2 units) with Sildenafil is generally tolerable; with Tadalafil's extended duration, even light drinking increases side effects. Moderation is advised.

Questions

Frequently asked about Sildenafil and Tadalafil

  • Which works faster: Sildenafil or Tadalafil?
    Both work in 30 minutes on average, but there are differences. Sildenafil (Viagra) typically works in 30-60 minutes, especially on an empty stomach. Tadalafil (Cialis) also works in 30 minutes but is less affected by food, making it more reliable when you've eaten. For spontaneous activity where timing is unpredictable, Tadalafil's food-independence is an advantage. For planned encounters, either works — the difference is minimal.
  • How long does Sildenafil last versus Tadalafil?
    Sildenafil lasts 4-6 hours — you take it 30-60 minutes before activity. Tadalafil lasts up to 36 hours — one dose covers you for 1-2 days. This is why Tadalafil is called "the weekend pill." For men who like spontaneity and can tolerate side effects for 36 hours, Tadalafil is superior. For men who prefer a defined window (knowing it wears off in 6 hours), Sildenafil is more predictable.
  • Can I take these medications if I'm on blood pressure medication?
    With caution. Both PDE5 inhibitors lower blood pressure and can interact dangerously with nitrates (nitroglycerin) — never combine. If you're on antihypertensives (amlodipine, lisinopril, etc.), discuss with your physician first. Blood pressure monitoring before and during treatment is essential. Absolute contraindications exist — your physician needs to review your medications before prescribing.
  • What are the side effects of Sildenafil vs Tadalafil?
    Sildenafil: Headache (20-30%), flushing, indigestion, nasal congestion. Tadalafil: Back pain (10-15%), headache, muscle aches, flushing. The back pain with Tadalafil is unique and more common at higher doses. Both cause blue-tinted vision at high doses (rare at therapeutic doses). Side effects are usually mild and decrease over time. Timing matters — Sildenafil side effects fade within 6 hours; Tadalafil's persist longer since it's in your system for 36 hours.
  • Is it safe to take these medications with alcohol?
    Theoretically possible but not recommended. Alcohol enhances the blood pressure-lowering effect, increasing dizziness, headache, and flushing risk. Light alcohol (1-2 drinks) with Sildenafil is less risky since it clears in 6 hours. With Tadalafil, even light alcohol with extended exposure (36 hours) increases side effect risk. If you drink, do so moderately and well-spaced from dosing. Your physician should advise based on your specific health profile.
  • Why are these medications so expensive in India?
    Sildenafil (Viagra brand) and Tadalafil (Cialis brand) are patent-protected — multinational brands command premium prices (300-800 INR per tablet).   • How do I know if I actually need these medications?
    You need blood tests first. 40% of men under 40 with ED have low testosterone or undiagnosed diabetes — the pill alone won't fix it. arq. tests testosterone, prolactin, glucose, lipids, and thyroid before prescribing. If your testosterone is low, you need testosterone replacement or clomiphene, not Sildenafil. If your glucose is high, you need diabetes management. The medication treats the symptom (erectile dysfunction); the bloodwork reveals the cause. Treating the cause while you take the medication gives you the best outcome.
  • Can I switch between Sildenafil and Tadalafil?
    Yes. Some men respond better to one than the other — this is normal individual variation in drug metabolism. If Sildenafil gives you too much headache, try Tadalafil. If Tadalafil's back pain is intolerable, switch to Sildenafil. Response rates are similar (70-80% efficacy in men with normal testosterone and no vascular disease), but side effect tolerance varies.
