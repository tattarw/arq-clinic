Tadalafil 5mg Daily: The Complete Guide to Daily ED Treatment

April 16, 2026
arq. medical team
Medically reviewed

Daily low-dose tadalafil is not the same as taking Tadalafil 20mg when you need it. Most men think ED treatment means popping a pill an hour before sex. But that's on-demand dosing. Daily tadalafil 5mg works differently — it maintains steady medication levels in your blood, so erectile function improves across your entire week, without planning ahead. This is especially useful for men with frequent sexual activity, performance anxiety, or benign prostatic hyperplasia (enlarged prostate). But like all ED medications, it requires understanding how it works, who it's for, and what safety checks come first.

Key takeaways

Before you start daily tadalafil 5mg:

  • Daily tadalafil 5mg is FDA-approved for ED and works through continuous PDE5 inhibition — no need to plan doses around sex
  • It reaches steady-state in 4-5 days and most men see results by week 2-3 of consistent dosing
  • NOT everyone needs daily dosing — if you have sex 2-3 times per week or less, on-demand 20mg is simpler
  • Bloodwork (testosterone, prolactin, lipids, glucose, blood pressure, kidney function) is non-negotiable before prescription
  • Absolute contraindication: Nitrates (for angina) + tadalafil = life-threatening hypotension. Period.
  • arq. requires full cardiovascular and metabolic workup before prescribing — because ED is often a warning sign of systemic disease

What is Daily Tadalafil and How Does It Work?

Tadalafil is a PDE5 inhibitor — it blocks phosphodiesterase type 5, an enzyme that breaks down cGMP in penile tissue. Why does this matter? cGMP relaxes smooth muscle in blood vessels, allowing blood to fill the corpus cavernosum (the erectile tissue in the penis). When you're sexually aroused, your nervous system releases nitric oxide, which triggers cGMP production. But PDE5 tears down cGMP, ending the erection. Tadalafil prevents this breakdown, so more cGMP persists, blood flow is easier, and erections are stronger and more reliable.

The difference between on-demand and daily dosing:

  • On-demand 20mg: You take a single dose 30-60 minutes before sex. It peaks in your blood, provides 5-6 hours of benefit, then wears off. Works for that single sexual encounter. You must plan ahead.
  • Daily 5mg: You take the same dose every day. It accumulates in your body, reaching steady-state after 4-5 days. From then on, you maintain consistent PDE5 inhibition 24/7. Erections happen more spontaneously, without planning. The medication is always there.

Key advantage: spontaneity. Daily tadalafil removes the mental burden of planning. You can be intimate whenever the moment arises — no pill to remember, no waiting 45 minutes for it to work.

Who Is Daily Tadalafil Right For?

Daily dosing is not for everyone. It's for specific men with specific patterns of sexual activity and specific medical situations.

Daily tadalafil 5mg works best for:

  • Men with frequent sexual activity (4+ times per week) — daily dosing is more convenient than repeated on-demand pills
  • Men with performance anxiety — the constant presence of medication reduces fear of erectile failure, breaking the anxiety cycle
  • Men with benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH) — tadalafil relaxes prostate and bladder muscle, improving urinary flow. Bonus: also treats ED
  • Men who value spontaneity — no planning, no waiting for medication to work
  • Men with psychogenic ED (anxiety, stress, relationship issues) — daily dosing often works better than on-demand because the psychological reassurance matters

On-demand 20mg is better for:

  • Men with infrequent sexual activity (1-3 times per week)
  • Men who want to take medication only when needed, not daily
  • Men concerned about side effects — on-demand 20mg is a single higher dose, but daily 5mg accumulates, so side effects may differ
  • Men who haven't had bloodwork yet — same safety checks apply, but on-demand is simpler to trial

The Mechanism: Steady-State PDE5 Inhibition

Understanding daily dosing requires understanding pharmacokinetics. Tadalafil has a long half-life: 17-21 hours (the longest of all PDE5 inhibitors). This matters.

When you take 5mg daily:

  • Day 1: You take 5mg. Levels rise, then fall by 50% after 17 hours.
  • Day 2: You take another 5mg. Baseline is already at 50% from day 1, so new dose adds to it. Levels climb higher.
  • Day 3-5: Levels continue accumulating. By day 4-5, you reach steady-state — input (new daily dose) equals output (elimination). Levels plateau.
  • Day 5 onward: Consistent levels, consistent PDE5 inhibition, consistent erectile benefit.

This is why most men don't see full benefit until week 2-3 — the drug needs time to accumulate. It's not like on-demand, where you feel it working after one dose.

The Blood Tests You Need Before Starting

ED is not just a sexual problem — it's often a window into cardiovascular and metabolic disease. Men with ED have higher rates of heart disease, diabetes, and hypertension. Some of these conditions are serious enough that tadalafil is contraindicated or requires caution. This is why bloodwork comes before prescription.

1. Total and Free Testosterone

Low testosterone causes ED independent of PDE5 enzyme activity. If your testosterone is below 300 ng/dL, tadalafil alone may not work — testosterone replacement might be needed first or alongside. Baseline testosterone is your reference point for monitoring.

2. Prolactin

High prolactin (above 17 ng/mL) suppresses testosterone and causes ED and low libido. If prolactin is elevated, your physician investigates the cause (pituitary adenoma, hypothyroidism, medication side effect) before prescribing tadalafil. Treating prolactin first is often necessary.

3. Glucose, HbA1c (Diabetes Screening)

Diabetes is the #1 cause of ED in men over 40 in India. If your blood glucose is elevated or HbA1c is above 5.7%, you have prediabetes or diabetes. Tadalafil can help, but glucose control must be addressed — uncontrolled diabetes means tadalafil may not work well, and your cardiovascular risk is high.

4. Lipid Panel (Total Cholesterol, HDL, LDL, Triglycerides)

High cholesterol and triglycerides impair blood vessel function, making ED worse. Tadalafil works by improving blood vessel function, but if your lipids are severely abnormal, you need medication for that too. Statin therapy often improves ED when cholesterol is the culprit.

5. Blood Pressure

Tadalafil lowers blood pressure slightly (typically 1-2 mmHg). If your baseline BP is already low or if you're on strong antihypertensive medications, tadalafil needs caution. If BP is elevated (above 160/100), it needs control before starting tadalafil.

6. Kidney Function (Creatinine, eGFR)

Tadalafil is cleared by the kidneys. If your eGFR is below 30 (advanced kidney disease), dose adjustment is mandatory. Most men with mild-moderate kidney disease tolerate standard dosing, but your physician must know your baseline.

7. Thyroid (TSH)

Hypothyroidism causes ED and low libido. If your TSH is elevated, thyroid hormone replacement is the first-line treatment for ED — tadalafil is secondary.

arq.'s approach: test comprehensively before prescribing. ED is often a symptom of undiagnosed diabetes, hypertension, or dyslipidemia. Bloodwork catches these, and your physician builds a protocol addressing all of them, not just ED.

Cardiovascular Safety: What You Must Know

The biggest question: is tadalafil safe for the heart? Short answer: yes, for most men, with caution.

The Physiology

Tadalafil works on vascular tissue — it relaxes smooth muscle in blood vessels everywhere (not just the penis). This is why it's approved for pulmonary hypertension and also for ED: it lowers blood pressure slightly and improves blood flow overall. This is usually beneficial for heart health. However:

  • If you're on nitrates: ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATION. Nitrates (nitroglycerin, isosorbide dinitrate) also work through cGMP pathways. Combined with tadalafil, they cause severe, life-threatening hypotension. Men with angina or heart failure on nitrates cannot take tadalafil. This is non-negotiable.
  • If you have uncontrolled hypertension or unstable angina: Tadalafil may be risky. Your BP and cardiac status must be stable before starting.
  • If you have severe aortic stenosis or severe left ventricular outflow obstruction: Tadalafil's blood-pressure-lowering effect could be dangerous.
  • For most other cardiac conditions (prior MI, stable angina, coronary artery disease, heart failure with reduced ejection fraction): Tadalafil can usually be used safely, often with benefit. In fact, tadalafil's blood pressure reduction may help. However, physician oversight is essential.

Sexual Activity and the Heart

Sexual activity itself raises heart rate and blood pressure. For men with significant cardiac disease, this is a risk. Tadalafil doesn't eliminate this risk — it actually makes sex easier by enabling erections, which means sexual exertion is more likely. Your cardiologist and ED physician need to communicate. If sex is contraindicated due to cardiac disease, tadalafil isn't the answer.

Drug Interactions: The Critical Ones

Nitrates — ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATION
Do not mix. Nitroglycerin, isosorbide dinitrate, isosorbide mononitrate. If you have angina and take nitrates, you cannot take tadalafil. Talk to your cardiologist about alternatives.

Alpha-blockers (for prostate/hypertension)
Alfuzosin, doxazosin, tamsulosin, terazosin. These lower blood pressure. Tadalafil also lowers BP. Combined, hypotension is possible. Most men tolerate the combination with monitoring, especially since tadalafil is often prescribed with alpha-blockers for BPH. Physician oversight is needed. Dosing may need adjustment.

Antihypertensive medications (ACE inhibitors, ARBs, beta-blockers, CCBs)
Tadalafil may enhance their blood-pressure-lowering effect. This is usually mild and often beneficial (better BP control), but your physician should know you're on both and monitor your BP.

CYP3A4 inhibitors (strong)
Medications that inhibit the CYP3A4 enzyme (which metabolizes tadalafil) increase tadalafil levels in your blood. Examples: ketoconazole, itraconazole, ritonavir. If you're on these, your tadalafil dose may need reduction. Inform your physician about all medications.

Grapefruit juice
Grapefruit inhibits CYP3A4. Large amounts can increase tadalafil levels. Occasional grapefruit is fine, but regular consumption should be avoided or discussed with your physician.

Side Effects at the 5mg Daily Dose

At daily 5mg, side effects are much milder than on-demand 20mg because the absolute drug exposure is lower (5mg/day vs 20mg once). Common side effects include:

  • Headache (5-10%): Mild to moderate, usually in first 2-4 weeks, then resolves. Some men take tadalafil in the evening to avoid daytime headache.
  • Indigestion/acid reflux (5-8%): Tadalafil can relax the lower esophageal sphincter. Mild, usually manageable with antacids or evening dosing.
  • Back/muscle pain (2-3%): Can be significant in some men, mild in others. Resolves by week 4 in most cases.
  • Flushing (1-2%): Facial redness, usually mild and transient.
  • Nasal congestion (2-4%): Tadalafil relaxes nasal blood vessels. Usually mild.
  • Vision changes (rare): Mild blur or blue-tinted vision. Reverse when drug clears. SIRE (sudden inexplicable reduction of eyesight) is extremely rare but documented — if you experience sudden vision loss, seek immediate medical attention.

Serious side effects (rare): Sudden sensorineural hearing loss, priapism (erection lasting >4 hours), severe hypotension, myocardial infarction (in men with undiagnosed cardiac disease or on nitrates). These are rare, but if they occur, seek emergency care.

Timeline: When Will You See Results?

Days 1-3: You take your first dose. You may not notice anything yet. The drug is accumulating, but levels are still low.

Days 4-7: Steady-state begins. PDE5 inhibition is now consistent. Some men notice improved erectile function by day 5-7, especially if ED is mild or psychogenic. Others notice nothing yet.

Weeks 2-3: This is when most men (70-80%) notice clear improvement. Erections are firmer, easier to achieve, and more spontaneous. Confidence starts returning. Some men see full benefit by week 3.

Week 4-6: Full benefit for most men. Erectile function has stabilized. Sexual confidence is higher, especially if performance anxiety was a factor. The psychological benefit — knowing medication is there — compounds the physiological effect.

Week 8+: Some men continue to see improvement as psychological confidence builds. By 8 weeks, you have a clear sense of whether daily tadalafil is working for you.

If you see no improvement by week 4-6, discuss with your physician. Options: increase to on-demand 20mg, add other therapies (testosterone if low, lifestyle changes), or investigate undiagnosed ED causes (relationship stress, psychiatric conditions, occult cardiac disease).

Tadalafil vs Sildenafil (Viagra) vs Vardenafil: Choosing the Right PDE5 Inhibitor

Tadalafil (Cialis, Tadacip)

  • Half-life: 17-21 hours (longest)
  • Daily dosing: Yes, 5mg approved and effective
  • On-demand dosing: Yes, 20mg available and effective
  • Side effects at daily 5mg: Minimal
  • Cost in India: 15-30 INR daily, cheapest long-term
  • Extra benefits: Also treats BPH (lower urinary tract symptoms)

Sildenafil (Viagra, Manforce)

  • Half-life: 4-5 hours (shorter)
  • Daily dosing: Not standard; 25mg daily exists but less common
  • On-demand dosing: Yes, 50-100mg, works well
  • Side effects: Higher at on-demand 100mg; headache, flushing common
  • Cost in India: 10-20 INR per on-demand dose, cheapest per-dose
  • Best for: Occasional sexual activity, quick action (30 min)

Vardenafil (Levitra, Zhewitra)

  • Half-life: 4-5 hours (short)
  • Daily dosing: Not standard
  • On-demand dosing: Yes, 5-20mg
  • Side effects: Similar to sildenafil
  • Cost: 15-35 INR per dose
  • Best for: Men who don't tolerate sildenafil or tadalafil

Why tadalafil for daily dosing? Its long half-life makes it ideal. Daily 5mg maintains therapeutic levels without accumulating to toxic levels. Sildenafil's shorter half-life means daily dosing requires higher cumulative exposure.
