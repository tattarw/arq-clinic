Questions

Frequently asked about Fatigue

What causes chronic fatigue?
Chronic fatigue has multiple treatable causes: iron deficiency, B12 deficiency, thyroid dysfunction, vitamin D insufficiency, adrenal dysregulation, blood sugar imbalance, and inflammatory markers. Most patients see multiple practitioners without comprehensive testing. arq. evaluates all 12 major markers to identify the root cause—not just symptoms. Once identified, fatigue often resolves within 8-12 weeks with targeted supplementation, lifestyle changes, or medication.
Why am I always tired?
Persistent tiredness usually stems from biochemical imbalance, not laziness. Common culprits: ferritin below 40 (even within 'normal' ranges), B12 deficiency, subclinical hypothyroidism, elevated morning cortisol, low vitamin D, and insulin resistance. Indian diets—often vegetarian—frequently lack bioavailable iron and B12. sleep debt compounds the issue. A comprehensive blood panel catches these; most standard checkups don't. Once markers are identified and treated, energy typically returns within weeks.
What blood tests diagnose fatigue?
Fatigue bloodwork must include: ferritin (iron stores), complete blood count (anemia), B12 and folate levels, TSH and free T3 (thyroid), vitamin D (25-OH), fasting glucose and HbA1c (blood sugar), morning and evening cortisol (adrenal function), and inflammatory markers (CRP, ESR). Standard annual checkups rarely test all of these. arq. runs a complete fatigue panel using NABL-accredited labs. Results are interpreted by physicians who understand optimal vs. 'normal' ranges—ferritin 40+ is optimal; below 30 causes fatigue despite lab-normal status.
Can iron deficiency cause fatigue?
Yes. Iron deficiency is the #1 nutritional cause of fatigue worldwide. Low ferritin (iron stores) reduces hemoglobin production, cutting oxygen delivery to muscles and brain. Women lose iron monthly; vegetarians and vegans struggle to absorb plant iron. Symptoms: exhaustion, shortness of breath, brain fog, hair loss. arq. measures both hemoglobin and ferritin. Many fatigued patients have normal hemoglobin but dangerously low ferritin. Supplementation (oral or IV) restores energy in 4-8 weeks. Testing ensures you get the right dose—too little doesn't help, too much causes constipation.
How does thyroid affect fatigue?
Thyroid hormone regulates metabolism and energy production in every cell. Low thyroid function (hypothyroidism) slows everything: metabolism drops, fatigue sets in, weight increases, mood darkens. In India, iodine deficiency and autoimmune thyroiditis are common. Many patients have 'subclinical' hypothyroidism—elevated TSH, low-normal free T3—causing fatigue without meeting treatment thresholds. arq. measures TSH, free T3, and thyroid antibodies. Your physician adjusts treatment until both symptoms and markers improve. Most patients feel better within 6-8 weeks of proper thyroid management.
What is B12 deficiency fatigue?
Vitamin B12 is essential for energy metabolism, red blood cell formation, and nerve function. Deficiency causes profound fatigue, weakness, brain fog, and numbness. Vegetarians, vegans, and those over 60 are at risk—stomach acid required for B12 absorption declines with age. arq. tests serum B12 and methylmalonic acid (the functional marker). Lab-normal B12 (200+) can still cause symptoms. B12 supplementation—oral or injections—quickly restores energy. Most patients report improved clarity and stamina within 4 weeks. Injections bypass absorption issues common in Indian populations.
How does cortisol affect energy?
Cortisol is your wake-up hormone—it should spike in the morning and fall by evening, allowing sleep. Chronic stress inverts this: low morning cortisol leaves you exhausted at 6 AM, high evening cortisol keeps you wired at 11 PM. This dysregulation is epidemic in India's high-pressure cities. arq. measures morning and evening cortisol to diagnose adrenal dysfunction. If cortisol is inverted or flat, lifestyle interventions (sleep, exercise, stress management) plus targeted supplements restore the rhythm. Most patients sleep and energy improve within 8-12 weeks as cortisol normalizes.
How does arq. diagnose and treat fatigue?
arq.'s process: (1) Physician consult to understand your fatigue pattern and history. (2) Comprehensive home blood draw testing 100+ biomarkers, including 12 fatigue-specific markers. (3) Results reviewed within 5 days; your physician identifies the root cause. (4) Personalized protocol combining supplements, lifestyle changes, and medication (if needed) tailored to your specific markers. arq. doesn't treat fatigue generically—your recommendations depend on whether you're low in iron, B12, thyroid, cortisol, or vitamin D. Most patients feel markedly better within 8 weeks.
