Frequently asked about Focus

What causes brain fog?
Brain fog stems from cortisol dysregulation (elevated AM cortisol), B12 deficiency (especially in vegetarians), subclinical hypothyroidism, vitamin D insufficiency, blood sugar dysregulation, and chronic inflammation. Most cognitive decline is metabolic, not neurological. People spend thousands on supplements, nootropics, or Modafinil without testing the fundamentals. arq. measures cortisol rhythm, B12 (methylmalonic acid), TSH/free T3, vitamin D, HbA1c, and inflammatory markers. Once the root is identified—elevated morning cortisol, B12 deficiency, or thyroid dysfunction—clarity returns within 8-12 weeks.
Is Modafinil safe and legal in India?
Modafinil is a prescription stimulant (Modiodal) available in India, approved for narcolepsy. Many professionals self-medicate for focus without testing why they're foggy. Modafinil works short-term by increasing dopamine and noradrenaline. But it doesn't address the root: if your cortisol is inverted or B12 is low, you'll remain dependent on Modafinil. arq.'s approach: test biomarkers first. If cortisol, B12, and thyroid are normal, you don't need Modafinil. If dysregulated, fix the root cause. Most patients restore natural focus within 8-12 weeks, eliminating the need for stimulants.
How does cortisol affect brain function?
Elevated morning cortisol (from chronic stress) impairs working memory, attention, and executive function. Cortisol dysregulation also disrupts dopamine production—critical for focus and motivation. When cortisol is high all day, your brain exhausts its neurotransmitter reserves. Afternoon fog and inability to concentrate result. arq. measures AM and PM cortisol. If elevated or inverted, stress management, exercise timing, and supplements restore cortisol rhythm. Once cortisol normalizes, most patients report dramatically improved clarity within 6-8 weeks. This is more effective than any nootropic.
Can blood tests detect brain fog causes?
Yes. Brain fog has measurable biochemical roots. arq. tests: cortisol (AM + PM), B12 and methylmalonic acid (functional B12 marker), TSH and free T3, vitamin D, fasting glucose and HbA1c, ferritin (oxygen delivery to brain), inflammatory markers (hs-CRP), and homocysteine (neurotoxic if elevated). Most people never get comprehensive cognitive bloodwork. GPs check a basic CBC and TSH; that misses 80% of causes. arq.'s complete panel identifies whether your fog stems from cortisol, B12, thyroid, glucose dysregulation, or inflammation. Treatment is targeted.
How does B12 deficiency cause brain fog?
B12 is essential for myelin production (nerve insulation), red blood cell formation, and energy metabolism in neurons. Deficiency causes cognitive decline, memory problems, concentration loss, and brain fog. In India, 40% of vegetarians have low B12 due to absent dietary sources. B12 is poorly absorbed via stomach acid; many people with lab-normal B12 still have functional deficiency. arq. tests serum B12 and methylmalonic acid (the functional marker). If low, B12 supplementation (oral, sublingual, or injections) restores clarity within 4-6 weeks. Most people notice sharper thinking and improved focus rapidly.
How does thyroid affect focus?
Thyroid hormone powers brain metabolism and neurotransmitter synthesis. Low thyroid function (hypothyroidism) slows neural firing; you think slowly, struggle to focus, and feel 'foggy.' Elevated thyroid causes racing thoughts and scattered attention. Many Indians have subclinical hypothyroidism—TSH elevated, free T3 low-normal—causing cognitive decline without obvious thyroid disease. arq. measures TSH, free T3, free T4, and thyroid antibodies. If thyroid is dysregulated, treatment restores mental clarity within 4-6 weeks. Most patients notice sharper thinking, faster processing, and restored focus.
Can blood sugar dysregulation cause brain fog?
Yes. High fasting glucose or HbA1c causes blood sugar spikes and crashes, which disrupt neurotransmitter production and brain energy. After meals, blood sugar spikes impair focus; during crashes, you feel exhausted and foggy. Insulin resistance from stress and processed foods is epidemic in India. You're sharp in the morning, completely foggy by 3 PM. arq. measures fasting glucose, HbA1c, and insulin levels. If dysregulated, dietary changes (lower glycemic load), exercise timing, and sometimes metformin stabilize blood sugar. Brain fog lifts within 2-4 weeks as glucose control improves. Mental clarity and sustained focus return.
