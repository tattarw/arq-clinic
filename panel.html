What is hs-CRP test—high sensitivity C-reactive protein?
High-sensitivity C-reactive protein (hs-CRP) measures inflammation—a root cause of most chronic diseases. Standard CRP detects acute infection; hs-CRP detects low-level chronic inflammation invisible to routine tests. hs-CRP below 1.0 mg/L is ideal; 1.0-3.0 indicates mild inflammation; above 3.0 suggests significant inflammation. Elevated hs-CRP correlates with cardiovascular disease, diabetes, cognitive decline, and poor recovery. arq. measures hs-CRP as a core marker; many members discover elevated inflammation despite feeling fine—prompting dietary or supplement interventions that prevent future disease.

Preventive testing shifts the paradigm from treating disease to preventing it.
