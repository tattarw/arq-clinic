How BPC-157 heals

BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound-157) is a 15-amino acid peptide originally isolated from human gastric juice. Its proposed mechanisms center on angiogenesis, nitric oxide pathway modulation, and growth factor upregulation — all critical processes in tissue repair and regeneration.

Angiogenesis
Promotes new blood vessel formation at injury sites, accelerating oxygen and nutrient delivery. Critical for tissue repair in tendons, ligaments, and gut.
Nitric Oxide Pathway
Modulates the NO system for vasodilation and improved blood flow. NO is central to endothelial function and tissue healing responses.
Growth Factor Upregulation
Increases VEGF (vascular endothelial growth factor), FGF (fibroblast growth factor), and other growth factors involved in tendon, ligament, and nerve repair.

FAQ

Common questions

What is BPC-157?
BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound-157) is a 15-amino acid peptide derived from human gastric juice. It has been studied for its potential to promote tissue healing, particularly in musculoskeletal and gastrointestinal systems. The compound gained popularity in biohacking communities due to promising pre-clinical research and anecdotal reports.
Is BPC-157 legal in India?
BPC-157 is not currently scheduled under Indian drug regulations (NDPS Act or Drugs and Cosmetics Act Schedule H/X). However, regulatory status is not the same as medical approval. It remains a research compound with no formal approval for human therapeutic use. Consult a healthcare provider to understand its legal and medical status for your specific use case.
How is BPC-157 administered?
In published research, BPC-157 has been studied via multiple routes: subcutaneous (SC) injection, oral administration, and intramuscular (IM) injection. Different routes may have different bioavailability and effects. Dosing protocols vary widely in research; consult your provider on appropriate administration for your situation.
What conditions is BPC-157 studied for?
Published studies explore BPC-157 for: tendon and ligament healing, musculoskeletal injuries, gastrointestinal ulcers and inflammation, gut barrier function, neuroprotection, and wound healing. Most evidence comes from pre-clinical animal models. Human clinical trial data remains limited. Discuss specific conditions with your healthcare provider.
What is the side effect profile?
In published research, BPC-157 has demonstrated favorable safety profiles with minimal reported adverse effects in animal studies. Human data is sparse. As a research peptide, comprehensive long-term safety data in humans is not available. Allergic reactions to the peptide or contaminants are theoretically possible. Always source from reputable providers and consult your healthcare provider before use.
