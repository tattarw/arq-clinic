Pharmacology

How modafinil works

Modafinil's mechanism of action is distinct from traditional stimulants. Rather than flooding the brain with dopamine like amphetamines, it works through a more nuanced pathway that promotes wakefulness without the jitteriness or crash.

Dopamine Reuptake
Inhibits DAT (dopamine transporter) at lower affinity than classical stimulants, producing wakefulness without euphoria or significant abuse potential.
Orexin/Hypocretin
Activates orexin neurons in the hypothalamus — the brain's master "wake" switch. This is the same pathway disrupted in narcolepsy.
Histamine & Norepinephrine
Increases histaminergic and noradrenergic signaling in wake-promoting brain regions, supporting sustained alertness and executive function.
Pharmacokinetics

How your body processes it

Modafinil is a racemic compound. The R-enantiomer (armodafinil) has a longer half-life and is primarily responsible for the extended duration of action. Understanding these parameters helps optimize dosing timing.

Bioavailability~80% (oral)
Tmax (time to peak)2–4 hours
Cmax (200mg dose)~4.6 µg/mL
Elimination half-life12–15 hours (R-enantiomer: ~15h, S-enantiomer: ~4h)
Steady state2–4 days
Volume of distribution~0.9 L/kg
Protein binding~62% (primarily albumin)
MetabolismHepatic — CYP3A4/5 (primary), CYP2C19. Moderate CYP3A4 inducer.
Active metaboliteModafinil acid (inactive), modafinil sulfone (weakly active)
ExcretionRenal (~80%), <10% excreted unchanged
Food effectDelays Tmax by ~1 hour. No significant effect on overall absorption (AUC).

Dosing

Optimal protocol

Your prescribing physician will determine the final dosing schedule. These are evidence-based guidelines commonly used in clinical practice and biohacking protocols.

Timing
Take once daily in the morning, ideally between 7–9 AM. The 12–15h half-life means a 7 AM dose clears significantly by 10 PM. Never take after 12 PM to protect sleep architecture.
RECOMMENDED
With food?
Can be taken with or without food. Food delays peak plasma levels by ~1 hour but doesn't reduce total absorption. Taking with a light meal may reduce nausea in sensitive individuals.
FLEXIBLE
Split dosing (advanced)
Some users prefer 100mg at 7 AM + 100mg at 11 AM for a more even plasma curve with less of a peak "kick." This approach may reduce headaches in sensitive individuals. Discuss with your physician.
ADVANCED
Cycling
Not pharmacologically required (no tolerance to wakefulness-promoting effects in clinical studies up to 40 weeks). However, many biohackers prefer 5-on/2-off (weekdays only) or 4-on/3-off to preserve subjective "sharpness" of response.

Evidence

Published research

Modafinil is one of the most-studied cognitive enhancers in existence. Below are key studies relevant to off-label cognitive use in healthy individuals.

2015 European Neuropsychopharmacology PMID: 26137838
Modafinil for cognitive neuroenhancement in healthy non-sleep-deprived subjects: A systematic review
Battleday & Brem reviewed 24 studies and found that modafinil consistently enhances attention, executive function, and learning in non-sleep-deprived individuals. Longer, more complex tasks showed the most benefit. Side effects were minimal and mood-related effects were negligible.
SYSTEMATIC REVIEW — 24 STUDIES
2008 Neuropsychopharmacology PMID: 17805308
A meta-analysis of the functional neuroimaging of modafinil
Minzenberg & Carter's meta-analysis of neuroimaging studies showed modafinil enhances executive function by modulating prefrontal cortex activity. Effects were most pronounced on tasks requiring cognitive control, working memory, and sustained attention.
META-ANALYSIS
2003 Psychopharmacology PMID: 12451432
Cognitive enhancing effects of modafinil in healthy volunteers
Turner et al. conducted a double-blind RCT in 60 healthy male volunteers. At 200mg, modafinil significantly improved digit span, visual pattern recognition memory, spatial planning (Tower of London task), and stop-signal reaction time vs. placebo.
RANDOMIZED CONTROLLED TRIAL — n=60
2004 Annals of Internal Medicine PMID: 15583228
Effects of modafinil on sleep-deprived performance: meta-analysis
Wesensten's analysis confirmed modafinil at 200–400mg significantly restores cognitive performance during sleep deprivation, with particular efficacy on executive function, vigilant attention, and working memory maintenance tasks.
META-ANALYSIS — SLEEP DEPRIVATION
2009 Journal of Clinical Psychopharmacology PMID: 19593178
Long-term efficacy and safety of modafinil for the treatment of excessive daytime sleepiness
40-week open-label extension study showed sustained wakefulness-promoting effects with no development of tolerance. Most common adverse events were headache (34%), nausea (11%), and anxiety (8%), with rates declining over time.

Interactions

Drug interactions

Modafinil is a moderate inducer of CYP3A4 and an inhibitor of CYP2C19. This affects the metabolism of several commonly used drugs. Your prescribing physician will review your full medication list.

!
Oral contraceptives (ethinyl estradiol)
Modafinil induces CYP3A4, which metabolizes ethinyl estradiol. This can reduce contraceptive efficacy by up to 18%. Use alternative or additional contraception during treatment and for one month after discontinuation.
Mechanism: CYP3A4 induction → increased estrogen clearance
!
Cyclosporine
CYP3A4 induction may reduce cyclosporine blood levels by 50%. Requires therapeutic drug monitoring if co-administered. Generally avoid combination.
Mechanism: CYP3A4 induction → decreased cyclosporine AUC
!
Warfarin
Modafinil inhibits CYP2C9, which metabolizes the S-enantiomer of warfarin. This may increase warfarin exposure and INR. Monitor INR more frequently if combining.
Mechanism: CYP2C9 inhibition → decreased S-warfarin clearance
i
Diazepam / Omeprazole (CYP2C19 substrates)
Modafinil inhibits CYP2C19, potentially increasing levels of drugs metabolized by this enzyme. Clinical significance varies — monitor for increased sedation with diazepam or increased acid suppression with omeprazole.
Mechanism: CYP2C19 inhibition → decreased substrate clearance
+
Caffeine
Synergistic wakefulness-promoting effects. No direct pharmacokinetic interaction, but combined use may increase anxiety, heart rate, or insomnia in sensitive individuals. Most users safely combine 100–200mg caffeine with 200mg modafinil. Consider reducing caffeine intake by 50% when starting.
Mechanism: Additive adenosine antagonism (caffeine) + DAT/orexin activation (modafinil)
