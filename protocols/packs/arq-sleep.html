Drug interactions

Sleep stack synergies

✓
Melatonin + All three (Mag + L-Theanine + Ashwagandha)
Synergistic. No pharmacokinetic competition. Each compound targets different sleep pathways: melatonin (circadian), magnesium (muscle/GABA), L-theanine (relaxation), ashwagandha (cortisol). Combined effect > sum of parts.
Mechanism: Different receptor systems; no CYP overlap; all oral, minimal absorption competition
⚠
Melatonin + Fluvoxamine (SSRI)
CYP1A2 inhibition: fluvoxamine can increase melatonin levels 12x. If taking fluvoxamine, reduce melatonin to 0.1-0.25mg. No dangerous interaction, but overdose risk is real.
Mechanism: Fluvoxamine inhibits CYP1A2 enzyme that metabolizes melatonin
⚠
L-Theanine + Antihypertensive medications
Mild additive blood pressure lowering possible. L-theanine slightly reduces BP; combined with BP meds may cause minor hypotension. Easily managed by dose timing or monitoring.
Mechanism: Both promote vasodilation and relaxation; additive effect unlikely to be significant
⚠
Ashwagandha + Thyroid medications
May enhance thyroid function. If taking levothyroxine or other thyroid meds, monitor TSH levels annually. Ashwagandha is mild thyroid stimulant; dose adjustment of thyroid med rarely needed.
Mechanism: Ashwagandha supports thyroid hormone metabolism
⚠
Magnesium + Antibiotics (Tetracyclines, Quinolones)
Mg2+ ions chelate these antibiotics, reducing efficacy. Separate dosing by 2+ hours (take antibiotic first, magnesium 2-4 hours later). Not a contraindication, just timing-dependent.
Mechanism: Magnesium cation chelates and reduces antibiotic absorption
