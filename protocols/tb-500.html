How TB-500 works

TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4) is a naturally occurring polypeptide with significant implications for tissue repair and regeneration. The peptide has been extensively studied in both systemic and localized applications, showing remarkable effects on cellular migration, angiogenesis, and anti-inflammatory pathways.

Actin Sequestration
Binds G-actin monomers, promoting cell migration and tissue repair at injury sites by regulating the actin cytoskeleton architecture.
Angiogenesis
Promotes new blood vessel formation, critical for healing tendons, ligaments, and muscle tissue. Stimulates endothelial cell migration and proliferation.
Anti-inflammatory
Downregulates inflammatory cytokines including TNF-α and IL-6, reducing pain and swelling at injury sites while promoting healing.

FAQ

Common questions

What is TB-500?
TB-500 is a synthetic peptide derived from Thymosin Beta-4, a naturally occurring protein in the human body. It consists of 43 amino acids and is studied for its regenerative and anti-inflammatory properties. It is particularly useful for musculoskeletal injuries, cardiac repair, and general tissue healing.
How is TB-500 different from BPC-157?
TB-500 and BPC-157 are complementary peptides with different mechanisms. BPC-157 is a 15-amino acid peptide that works primarily at the local injection site, promoting angiogenesis and collagen synthesis. TB-500 is a larger 43-amino acid peptide with systemic effects, working through actin binding and broader anti-inflammatory pathways. Many users stack them for enhanced synergistic healing.
What is the legal status of TB-500 in India?
TB-500 is not yet scheduled under Indian pharmaceutical law. As a research-grade peptide, it exists in a regulatory gray area. It is not approved for human therapeutic use but is widely available for research purposes. Consult with a physician regarding its use in your specific situation.
What is the typical administration protocol?
Standard protocols involve subcutaneous injections of 2–10 mg per injection, administered 2–3 times per week. The vial is typically reconstituted with bacteriostatic water and stored refrigerated. Duration depends on the injury; most protocols run 8–12 weeks with follow-up assessments. Your physician will determine the optimal dosing for your specific needs.
Can I stack TB-500 with BPC-157?
Yes, stacking TB-500 with BPC-157 is common and is supported by research showing synergistic effects. Many users inject TB-500 systemically or near the injury site 2–3 times per week, and inject BPC-157 locally at the injury site 1–2 times per week. This combined approach often produces superior healing outcomes for musculoskeletal injuries.
