Pharmacology

How tretinoin works

Tretinoin (all-trans retinoic acid) binds to retinoid receptors in the nucleus, directly modifying gene expression to accelerate cell turnover and stimulate collagen synthesis. It's not a symptom mask—it literally rebuilds skin at the cellular level.

Retinoid Receptor Activation
Binds RAR (retinoic acid receptors), increasing cell turnover 2-3x and accelerating the replacement of photodamaged skin with fresh, healthy cells.
Collagen & Elastin Synthesis
Stimulates production of collagen I and III, and increases elastin. Studies show 80% increase in collagen after 12 weeks of use.
Melanin Regulation
Inhibits tyrosinase activity and increases melanin clearance, fading hyperpigmentation and melasma over time.

Dosing

Optimal protocol

Tretinoin must be titrated slowly to minimize irritation and allow skin adaptation (retinization). Consistent nightly dosing with proper sun protection is key to safety and efficacy.

Retinization Schedule
Week 1–2: Every 3rd night (0.025% or 0.05%). Week 3–4: Every other night (0.025%). Week 5+: Nightly (0.025–0.1%). Slow titration prevents irritation, peeling, and redness. Start low-strength, increase only if well-tolerated. Patience is essential.
CRITICAL
Application Amount
Pea-sized amount per application (about 0.5mg cream or gel). Apply to clean, completely dry face. Do NOT apply to wet skin (increases irritation). Let skin dry 20 minutes after cleansing before applying tretinoin.
PEA-SIZED
Sunscreen (MANDATORY)
SPF 30+ broad-spectrum every morning (even indoors). Tretinoin increases photosensitivity 3–4x. UV exposure causes photodamage, reduces efficacy, and increases burning risk. Sunscreen is NOT optional — critical for safety and results.
MANDATORY
Skin Type & Strength
Sensitive skin: 0.025% cream (most tolerable). Normal skin: 0.05% cream. Oily/thick skin: 0.1% gel (absorbs faster). Creams > gels for sensitive users (slower penetration). Increase strength only after 8+ weeks at current dose, if tolerating well.

Evidence

Published research

Tretinoin is the gold standard topical retinoid with five decades of clinical evidence. Landmark studies consistently demonstrate efficacy on photoaging, fine wrinkles, pigmentation, and acne.

1986 Journal of the American Academy of Dermatology PMID: 3543767
Kligman et al. — Landmark Photoaging Study
Pioneering study demonstrating tretinoin efficacy in photoaged skin. Tretinoin increased collagen synthesis by 80% and improved fine wrinkles, tactile roughness, and skin thickness after 24 weeks. Established tretinoin as gold-standard for photoaging.

Randomized controlled trial: tretinoin 0.05% nightly achieved 68% improvement in fine wrinkles at 48 weeks. Coarse wrinkles showed 50% improvement. Results sustained for 12 months post-treatment. Confirmed sustained benefit of tretinoin with proper sun protection.

Safety

Side effects & safety

Tretinoin's side effects are primarily local (retinization) and temporary. Serious adverse events are extremely rare with topical use. Most side effects resolve in 4–12 weeks as skin adapts.

Important safety notes
TERATOGENIC: Topical tretinoin is Category C (pregnancy risk cannot be ruled out). Avoid if pregnant or planning pregnancy. Systemic absorption is minimal (<2%), but exercise caution. Retinization (peeling/redness) is temporary; typically resolves within 4–12 weeks. Avoid concurrent use of other irritating agents initially. Sunscreen is mandatory due to increased photosensitivity.
Interactions

Drug interactions

Tretinoin is minimally absorbed systemically (<2%), so systemic drug interactions are rare. Primary concerns are topical interactions with other skincare ingredients.

!
Benzoyl Peroxide
AVOID concurrent use: Benzoyl peroxide oxidizes and inactivates tretinoin, rendering it ineffective. If using both, apply benzoyl peroxide in AM and tretinoin at night, with 8+ hour separation. Better to use one at a time.
Mechanism: Oxidative inactivation of tretinoin
!
Other Drying Agents (Salicylic Acid, AHAs, BHAs)
CAUTION: Combining tretinoin with AHAs/BHAs/salicylic acid increases irritation, redness, and peeling during retinization. Avoid combining initially; introduce other actives only after skin fully adapts (8+ weeks).
Mechanism: Additive irritation and desquamation
!
Vitamin C (L-Ascorbic Acid) Serums
CAUTION: High pH vitamin C serums can inactivate tretinoin. Use tretinoin at night; vitamin C in AM only (after 20+ minutes drying time). Or use stabilized vitamin C (magnesium ascorbyl phosphate) if combining, but spacing is safer.
Mechanism: pH incompatibility reduces tretinoin stability
i
Sunscreen (Non-Reactive)
No interaction. Sunscreen is synergistic and essential. Apply sunscreen 15 minutes AFTER tretinoin (on fully dry skin). Most sunscreens are chemically compatible; mineral sunscreens are safest for sensitive skin.
Mechanism: No interaction; synergistic protection
+
Niacinamide / Ceramides (Moisturizers)
Synergistic benefit: Niacinamide and ceramides reduce tretinoin irritation while maintaining efficacy. Using a niacinamide + ceramide moisturizer during retinization reduces peeling and improves tolerance. Recommended adjunct.
Mechanism: Barrier support + irritation reduction
FAQ

Common questions

When will I see results?
Initial benefits like improved skin smoothness appear in 4-6 weeks. Fine line reduction and texture improvement visible by 8-12 weeks. Significant collagen-building effects emerge at 3-6 months. Hyperpigmentation fading takes 3-6 months or longer.
What is the purging phase?
Tretinoin increases skin cell turnover, which can temporarily bring deep comedones to the surface as breakouts. This "purging" typically lasts 4-8 weeks and is a sign the retinoid is working. It resolves as your skin adapts.
Can I use it in summer?
Yes, but with strict sun protection. Tretinoin increases photosensitivity, so broad-spectrum SPF 30+ is mandatory daily, even on cloudy days. Many dermatologists recommend starting in winter for easier tolerance.
Is tretinoin safe during pregnancy?
Tretinoin is contraindicated during pregnancy due to teratogenic risk (birth defects). If planning pregnancy or already pregnant, discontinue tretinoin and consult your OB/GYN. Switch to safer alternatives like azelaic acid.
How to layer with other products?
Apply tretinoin to completely dry skin after your cleanser. Wait 10-15 minutes before applying other actives or moisturizer. Avoid combining with vitamin C, AHA/BHA, or benzoyl peroxide initially. Use only gentle, hydrating products alongside tretinoin.
