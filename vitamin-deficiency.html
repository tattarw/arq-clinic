Questions

Frequently asked about Vitamin Deficiency

What are the symptoms of vitamin D deficiency?
Vitamin D deficiency symptoms include fatigue, muscle weakness, bone pain, depression, and impaired wound healing. Many people experience mood changes, particularly seasonal affective symptoms. In children, deficiency can lead to rickets. Since symptoms often overlap with other conditions, blood testing is the only reliable way to confirm deficiency and establish your baseline.
How is B12 deficiency treated in India?
B12 deficiency treatment depends on the cause. Most people benefit from injections (monthly or quarterly) or high-dose oral supplements. The specific protocol—injection frequency, dosage, and duration—depends on your serum B12 level, homocysteine, and methylmalonic acid markers. At arq., your physician prescribes exactly what your markers indicate, not a generic approach.
How do I increase ferritin levels naturally?
Ferritin increases through iron-rich foods (red meat, spinach, lentils) and, more effectively, prescribed iron supplements. However, supplementing without testing can cause harm. Your physician first determines if low ferritin is from iron deficiency anemia or other causes, then prescribes the exact dose and type of iron you need—oral, IV, or combination therapy.
What are the symptoms of iron deficiency anemia in India?
Iron deficiency anemia causes fatigue, shortness of breath, pale skin, brittle nails, and hair loss. Women of reproductive age are especially vulnerable due to menstrual losses. Many people attribute these symptoms to stress or aging. Blood tests measuring hemoglobin, ferritin, and iron saturation reveal the true cause and severity, guiding targeted treatment.
Should I take supplements without testing?
No. Supplementing without blood tests risks harmful overconsumption. High iron can damage the liver and heart. Excess vitamin D raises calcium levels dangerously. Even B12 can mask underlying neurological damage if the real cause isn't addressed. arq. tests first to identify your exact deficiency, then prescribes the precise dose you need—preventing both deficiency and toxicity.
What is the normal vitamin D level in India?
Most health authorities recommend vitamin D levels above 30 ng/mL (75 nmol/L) for general health, with optimal levels at 40-60 ng/mL for bone and immune function. India's average is significantly below these thresholds due to dietary insufficiency, limited sun exposure, and darker skin pigmentation reducing synthesis. arq. measures your exact level and builds a protocol to achieve your individual optimal range.
What causes vitamin B12 deficiency?
B12 deficiency stems from dietary insufficiency (vegetarian/vegan diets), poor absorption (pernicious anemia, stomach surgery, IBS), or impaired metabolism (genetic mutations like MTHFR). Your serum B12 level alone doesn't reveal the cause. arq. tests B12, homocysteine, methylmalonic acid, and intrinsic factor antibodies to identify the root cause and prescribe targeted treatment.
How does arq. test vitamin levels?
arq. draws blood at your home and sends it to NABL-accredited labs for comprehensive testing of over 100 biomarkers. For vitamin deficiencies, we measure serum levels, storage markers (ferritin), functional markers (homocysteine, methylmalonic acid), and absorption indicators. Your physician integrates all markers to diagnose the root cause, then prescribes a personalized protocol within 48 hours.
