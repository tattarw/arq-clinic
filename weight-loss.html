Can I get Semaglutide without a prescription in India?
No. Under Indian pharmaceutical regulations (Drugs and Cosmetics Act), semaglutide is a Schedule H drug and legally requires a valid prescription from a registered medical practitioner. Many online platforms and black markets in India sell semaglutide without prescriptions, but this is illegal and unsafe. Ozempic and Wegovy are both semaglutide but marketed differently. Ozempic is FDA-approved for type 2 diabetes in the U.S. and approved for diabetes in India. Is Tirzepatide (Mounjaro) available in India?

Before GLP-1 therapy, essential tests include: HbA1c (baseline glucose control), fasting insulin (insulin resistance assessment), TSH and free T3/T4 (rule out thyroid-driven weight gain), liver enzymes (ALT, AST, GGT—GLP-1s are hepatically metabolized), lipid panel (track cardiovascular risk), and amylase/lipase (safety screen for pancreatitis). arq. runs 100+ biomarkers including these and others (inflammatory markers, kidney function, etc.) to identify the root cause of your weight gain, not just prescribe medication blindly. Are GLP-1 medications safe for long-term use?
GLP-1 medications are safe for long-term use when properly monitored. Clinical trials show sustained weight loss over 2+ years with good tolerability. Common side effects (nausea, vomiting, constipation) are dose-dependent and usually subside. Rare serious risks include pancreatitis and thyroid concerns in those with family history of medullary thyroid cancer. This is why baseline testing matters: arq. physicians screen for pancreatitis risk (amylase/lipase), thyroid disease (TSH, free T3/T4), and liver impairment before prescribing.
